\caption{Estimation pipeline.
\textbf{Top row}: raw 1-minute prices are tick-scaled to integer units, missing
bars are filled by last-observation-carried-forward (zero price change), and
lag-$\tau$ increments $\delta_\tau(t)=P(t)-P(t{-}\tau)$ are formed on two
log-spaced grids.
The empirical PDF $\hat{P}(x_j,\tau)$ is constructed at each lag using
histogram bin width $\Delta x=\varepsilon\Delta$, with $\varepsilon$ varied
across five values as a robustness dimension.
\textbf{Middle}: three independent estimators are applied to the same PDF.
(1)~Tip-scaling OLS regresses $\log\hat{P}(0,\tau)$ on $\log\tau$ and reads
off $\hat\alpha_{\mathrm{tip}}=-1/\hat{m}$.
(2)~PDF collapse minimises the cross-$\tau$ variance of the rescaled density
$\tilde{P}(\tilde{x},\tau)$ over a grid of trial exponents.
(3)~Structure functions compute the $q$-th absolute moment $\hat{S}_q(\tau)$
at each lag; the Lévy scaling $\hat{S}_q\!\propto\!\tau^{q/\alpha}$ gives
$\hat\alpha_q$ per moment order, and the median is taken.
\textbf{Bottom row} (violet, dashed): lag-$k$ autocorrelations $\hat\rho(k)$
from the 1-minute series form the variance ratio $\widehat{VR}(\tau)$, whose
log yields the bias term $b(\tau)=-\tfrac{1}{2}\log\widehat{VR}(\tau)$.
Subtracting $b(\tau)$ from $\log\hat{P}(0,\tau)$ and re-running the OLS
with the coefficient of $b$ constrained to one gives the
autocorrelation-corrected estimate $\hat\alpha_{\mathrm{corr}}$.}
\label{fig:pipeline}
